DistanceCS CodeSystem

Measurement needs to be done to calculate the distance between two reference point or a lesion location (mass) to a reference point on the troubled body organ with the aim of deriving an accurate position of the mass on the organ to facilitate the appropriate treatment should be done, while also aiming at analyzing changes in the organ's dynamic morphology before, after, and during the treatment to maintains normal stable position of the organs.

★ Distance measurement between two reference point of breast morphology

Pic 1. Linear-Distance and Through-skin between two referencence point Measurement of Breast

The horizontal red lines indicate the level of the sternal notch (胸骨), the upper margin of nipple base, medial margin of the nipple, the lower margin of nipple base and the inframammary fold (乳房下皱襞). The vertical lines indicate the anterior midline (前正中線) and the middle line of the nipple. The point on the pic 3 above describe as the intersection of the horizontal and vertical red lines and be known as:

♢ A = sternal notch;
♢ A’ = the upper margin of the nipple base;
♢ B = anterior midline;
♢ B’ = nipple;
♢ C’ = the lower margin of the nipple base;
♢ C = the lowest point of the inframammary fold;
♢ D = the intersection of the anterior midline and the lowest level of the inframammary fold;

Thus, the black line is represented as distance measurement between points, as described below:

♢ AA’ = the distance between sternal notch to the upper margin of the nipple base;
♢ BB’ = the distance between anterior midline to the nipple;
♢ CC’ = the distance between the lower margin of the nipple base to the lowest point of the inframammary fold;
♢ AD = the distance between the sternal notch to the lowest point of the inframammary fold;
